Customization pipeline for low-cost, personalized orthosis fabrication

More than 1.3 million U.S. adults have rheumatoid arthritis, a painful inflammation of the joints in the hands and fingers. The condition is characterized by hyperflexion which is when a joint has an abnormal range of motion.

Patients design their own orthosis. Rather than requiring a doctor to examine a patient’s arthritis symptoms, prescribe a treatment, and regular check in on the patient’s condition, which can be costly and difficult to access for many people, we attempted to create a cost-efficient, optimized pipeline for patients to customize their own orthoses, with multiple pathways to account for accessibility to technology and medical resources.

This finger brace is designed to treat arthritis by straightening the affected finger(s), and allowing it to bend freely for daily activities.
